simulation-engine.ts
The core of the advantage. Three calibrated variance levels per simulation:
Game-level — samples a single factor for the entire matchup (NBA: ±10%, NFL: ±18%, MLB: ±25%). In a "shootout" game script, every player in that game gets amplified. In a grind, everyone gets compressed.
Team-level — within that game, samples which team ran the script. A 1.4× team factor means they controlled the game — their pass-catchers, hitters, and forwards all benefit proportionally.
Player-level — individual idiosyncratic variance, calibrated by position. NFL WRs have a 55% CV (very boom/bust), QBs 22% (more consistent), DSTs 60% (shutout or disaster). MLB hitters 65% (binary hit/HR outcomes). NHL forwards 62%.
NFL QB Cascade — the key differentiator. When a QB simulates at 1.4× median, his WRs receive 40% of that upside, TEs 35%, RBs 8%. This means when a QB stacks, his pass-catchers follow him organically in the same simulation — the LP sees consistent projections and naturally selects the stack without any rules.
MLB/NHL stacking — additional secondary team correlation for batters and NHL forward lines ensures teammates cluster in high-scoring game scripts.

optimizer-route.ts — /api/optimize/sim
Runs numSims LP solves (one per game script). Each LP call sees that sim's correlated projections. Results are deduplicated across all sims. Then every unique lineup is scored across all simulations (not just the ones it won), computing:

avgSimScore — average across all sims
p75Score — 75th percentile ceiling
p90Score — 90th percentile max upside
freqPct — percentage of sims this lineup was the LP-optimal result (a consensus signal)
compositeScore — 0.35×avg + 0.35×p75 + 0.20×p90 + 0.10×frequency

Lineups are ranked by composite score, then exposure caps are applied. Returns exposureSummary showing each player's appearance rate across the selected portfolio.
